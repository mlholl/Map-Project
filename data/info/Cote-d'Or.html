Welcome to Cote-d'Or!

The two sub-regions of Cote-d'Or are:

  1. Cote de Nuits (north)
  2. Cote de Beaune (south)

The narrow region is only 31 miles long and is located in the Burgundy region of France. It is home to a population of a little over 500,000 people.

While most of the white wines produced in the Cote-d'Or are made from 100% Chardonnay grapes, the red grape, Gamay, is exclusive to Beaujolais. In the Cote-d'Or, the main red grape used to produce red wines is the one and only Pinot Noir.
